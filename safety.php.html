Safety

Student Aircraft Builders is committed to establishing a stable organization which will allow continued growth for years to come. As a result of this, safety is of paramount concern. The image of our group and the continued support of our university and sponsors could be damaged should any accident occur. To prevent this, extensive measures have been taken to ensure that such an event will not occur.

Workshop Safety

Of great importance in the near future is workshop safety. All construction will take place under the direction of one of three construction managers who are extensively trained with the tools equipped at the hangar and the construction techniques involved. Safety equipment, general or relevant to any construction taking place during that day, will be required by anyone wishing to enter the workshop with sanctions being placed on violators. Access to certain tools, such as the larger and high powered machinery, will be restricted to authorized build managers.

Quality Assured Construction

We eventually plan on flying this plane, so we want to be completely certain that every step is completed to perfection. Fortunately, the construction plans are very specific. To assist with any ambiguities that may arise in the plan, we possess access to a range of supplemental materials created to assist aircraft homebuilders. To prevent possible mistakes, we require all construction to be checked by each build manager. We believe that this will guarantee the integrity of the aircraft.

Certification of Completed Aircraft

To prove the airworthiness of our completed aircraft, we will need to complete a program that involves putting the aircraft through its paces. Current FAA regulations allow private certified builders to conduct their own test program. However, as the vast majority of all aircraft accidents occur as a result of pilot error, we plan on employing a test pilot to conduct the first stage of the certification hours. Out of concern for the decision making of the pilot, there will not be an audience during the certification process. True first flight audiences have the power to distract the pilot from the task at hand. To prevent this, our announced first flight will be following full certification.
