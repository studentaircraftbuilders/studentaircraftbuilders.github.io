Long Term Goals

Our first long term goal is to take home the AirVenture 2014, Homebuilt Lindy Aircraft Award. Following this, we plan on participating in the 2015 Sun-n-Fun Fly-In in Lakeland Florida. From there, we plan on venturing into FAA certified experimental aircraft modifications.

The first area we are interested in is alternative aircraft propulsion. Alternative propulsion has a very wide range of possibilities, from using biofuels or natural gas instead of traditional petroleum or diesel fuels, to hydrogen or a hydrogen-petrol mix, and electric propulsion methods. We believe large changes need to come to the field of aviation, primarily in efficiency and cost. Electric aviation poses to be the future, with great advancements being made by NASA and teams like Pipistrel. We strive to leave our mark in this emerging field.

Other areas we find important are advances in turbulence damping and creating the first full instrument landing system (ILS). This would mean a FAA Category III C instrument landing system. This is a precision instrument approach and landing with no decision height and no runway visual range limitations. This category is not yet in operation anywhere in the world, as it requires guidance to taxi in zero visibility. Category III B is currently the best available system.

After constructing the Zenith, which requires approximately 1,500 hours of active construction to complete, we will have enough experience in aircraft construction to comfortably begin construction on other, less conventional small aircraft designs. Proposed designs include Burt Rutan's VariEze and Long-EZ, or possibly Aircraft Spruce's four seater Cozy. All of these time tested models incorporate composite materials in their construction, and will allow our members to gain experience with those composites.
