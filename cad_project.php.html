CAD Project

The Computer Aided Design (CAD) Project spawned from the idea that SAB needed some type of physical representation of where a sponsor's logo would be on the finished plane. After messing around for an hour or two, we had a rough model that looked similar to a finished RV-7A that would serve as an example as to where each tier of logo would go. From this model, we came up with the idea of teaching a CAD program to our members, specifically those in the Aerospace Department who have no experience with any type Computer Aided Design as there is currently no class dedicated to teaching them.

From this idea we morphed the program from just a teaching aspect into something that would be more directly beneficial in the short and long run. We decided we were going to CAD the plane in its entirety, down to every piece of sheet metal. For this we would need a large number of members in order to not be overloaded. Students are always very interested in gaining marketable skills, and they have proved that tendency with us. We are very pleased with our success and the program will only continue to grow as the next school year rolls around.

The idea behind CADing the plane serves 4 purposes:

  1. Draw in new members as an easy, fun, and non-time consuming project that gives them an opportunity to work with the plane and allows for advancement through the organization in the years to come.
  2. Teach students different CAD programs. Currently the Mechanical Department teaches Pro Engineer/Wildfire 5.0, so we will be able to draw strongly from those skilled students, but we will also be teaching NX 8.0, drawing from the experience of students and faculty with industry experience in NX.
  3. Assist our Project Manager in creating build directions by providing him with part drawings so he can give a physical representation of the directions and show exactly how pieces are to go together.
  4. Be able to run Finite Element Analysis on the finished model for future research into plane design.

The project was started mid-semester last spring, and the foundations are laid as we move through the summer and into the next school year. We are teaching our members using short videos of each function as well as personal teaching from other students to answer any questions the members have when learning to CAD. This teaching model was tested briefly before the year ended, and worked very well in the limited amount of time we had. Members were able go at their own pace and skip back and forth through the video in case they forgot how to do something, while student teachers assisted in any questions or difficulties they were having.
