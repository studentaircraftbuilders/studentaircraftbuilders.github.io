Design Team

The former NX and ProEngineer CAD Teams have integrated into the new Design Team that will be designing a small scale semi-glider (two meter wingspan). Included in this design is a launch system that will get the glider to altitude, a power system that will increase flight time, and an avionics system to send and receive data from the glider. After design is completed in the spring, the design team hopes to build the glider in the fall. The long-term goal of the design team is to use this experience to learn the hurdles of designing an aircraft and establish a framework for future SAB design challenges.

The top level concept for this semester is a bio-diesel powered glider with a two meter wingspan which will achieve a glide ratio of thirty. It will weigh under three kilograms and will be ground launched. We'll have an onboard camera and inertial measurement unit (IMU), providing data which will be relayed back through the avionics system to our ground control. Our glider this semester is a fairly modest project to introduce the concept of design to the team. Next semester we will be building and testing the glider.
