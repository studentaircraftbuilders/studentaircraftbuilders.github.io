Bio of Team SAB

SAB was founded by friends has been a fraternal effort. SAB should be as friendly and social and inspiring as possible. The origins of the project speak well to the atmosphere that should surround the project: "Erik Lopez's Super Secret Project." All the fun, goofing, mystery, and intrigue of "Erik Lopez's Super Secret Project"should not be lost in the bureaucracy that running SAB entails.

We, college students, young 20-somethings, rather inexperienced airplane builders, are building an airplane. How cool is that? Mistakes will be made, obstacles will be circumvented, but we are all learning together.

SAB welcomes novice. SAB is structured so that everyone is qualified. All that SAB asks is that a person come into the organization ready to work, learn, and have fun. SAB was created so that students would have a chance to take on a hands-on project. What could be more hands-on than constructing an airplane?

Tim Pekau

and Zak Lee Richardson

Design Team Leads

We are the joint heads on of SAB Design Team. Together we oversee and provide direction to the project. We accomplish this through out weekly meetings where we sit down individually with each team and talk about progress made during the previous week and progress to be made over the next week. We also are responsible for the top level concept of the project, i.e. broad design constraints intended to provide a direction for the team as a whole and a set of guidelines each individual subsystem needs to fulfill. Lastly, we review each team's decisions and decide whether their progress is in line with what we want.

Contact Zak and Tim at leerich2@illinois.edu and pekau2@illinois.edu respectively.
