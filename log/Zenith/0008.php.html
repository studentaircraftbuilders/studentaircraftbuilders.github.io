January 15, 2015

Finishing the Right Slat and Starting the Left Inboard Slat

Builders: Brian Read, Amy Zou, Suraj Bansal
Foreman: Jessie Lauzon, Przemyslaw Rostkowski

Picking up where we left off the day before, we deburred the ribs, corner doubler, and entire 75S1-3 sheet (the right outboard slat). Then, we riveted the ribs and corner doubler to the sheet. During the previous build session, there was a hole in a rib that was slightly bigger, but we chose to rivet the hole with the same size rivet (that fits drill size 30). Below is a picture of the result.

A mistake was made when riveting one of the ribs. We riveted the wrong way (oops!). We were able to drill the rivet out, but the resulting hole was large enough to require drilling to size 20 and using a larger rivet. The before and after pictures are below.

After riveting the ribs, we bent the sheet with the help of ratchet straps and a ruler to start matching drilling the ribs to the other side of the slat. The remaining holes were also match drilled and enlarged to size 30. We unfolded the sheet and deburred everything. We kept the ratchet straps on, but loosened, while deburring to save time and effort when refolding the piece for riveting.

Before riveting, Amy had the forethought to slide the finished right inboard slat piece inside the piece in-work. We would’ve had to do this eventually, but doing so before riveting was a great idea because the pieces fit very snug together and fitting them together after riveting may have proved difficult.

We then riveted the outboard slat, which concluded the assembly of the right slat.

To start the left slat assembly, Amy marked the parts to match the project management instructions. We then put together four more ribs assemblies. The parts were already cut from a previous build session. The ribs were then cleco-ed to the left inboard slat slat sheet, match drilled, and then drilled to size 30.

The next build session should start off de-burring the ribs and sheet, then riveting.
