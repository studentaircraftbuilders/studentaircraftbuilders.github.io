January 17, 2015

Attempt at Drilling the Ribs on Left Outboard Slat

Builders: Brian Read, Amy Zou, Suraj Bansal
Foreman: Jessie Lauzon

We were able to match drill the holes of the ribs to the left outboard slat sheet based on our corrections from the day before. However, when we went to drill to size 30, the last two holes on the middle rib had become mis-aligned again. Since the holes were already slightly deformed from the match-drilling, and enlarging the hole again would deform the hole even more, we felt that if we went agead and drilled again we would end up needing a new rib. We had several ideas to try to match the holes, including poking a rod through an adjacent hole and trying to push the rib into place, but that didn't work (and the idea had some flaws, too). We decided to call it a day and have a few more people see the issue and perhaps throw some new ideas out. Below is a representation of the issure that we're having. A slightly mis-matched hole is fixable by drilling to size 20 and using an A5 blind river, but we think this issue is more than slightly mis-matched.

Overall, it was a rather disappointing day since we were so close to finishing the slats, but we never thought building the Zenith would be without challenges!
