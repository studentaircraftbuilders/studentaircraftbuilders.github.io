January 16, 2015

Finishing the Left Inboard Slat and Starting the Left Outboard Slat

Builders: Brian Read, Amy Zou, Suraj Bansal
Foreman: Jessie Lauzon, Przemyslaw Rostkowski

For the left inboard slat, the ribs were uncleco-ed, everything deburred, and then the ribs were riveted to the sheet. We folded the sheet over with the help of ratchet straps and match drilled the other side of the ribs, where the sheet folded on itself. After enlarging the holes to the final size, we deburred everything and then riveted everything together. This finished the left inboard slat. The configuration of the straps is shown below, in case it helps in the future.

The last piece of the slats, the left outboard slat, was started by riveting one side of the ribs to the sheet per the usual procedure. After folding the sheet over, the holes on the other side of the ribs(the unriveted holes) were matching up poorly with the holes on the sheet. We uncleco-ed everything and put it back together again, this time trying to cleco the ribs first. This helped a little. The holes were still not perfect, but seemed acceptable. This is where we left off for the day.
