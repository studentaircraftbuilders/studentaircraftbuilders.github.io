January 14, 2015

Finishing the Right Inboard Slat and Starting the Right Outboard Slat

Builders: Brian Read, Amy Zhou
Foreman: Jessie Lauzon

Taking off from where we left the project the day before, the first steps were to de-burr the holes connecting the rib assemblies and corner doubler to the bottom of 75S1-2 (the inboard right slat sheet). We then riveted those holes, starting with the corner doubler and moving our way up the ribs. We then folded the sheet so we could cleco the ribs to the top of the slat. We ere able to use ratchet straps to help fold the sheet over and a ruler to help slide the bottom of 75S1-2 underneath the top. The following pictures show the result.

After several tries, the top of the slat (pictured as the bottom surface in Figure 2) was cleco-ed to the ribs. The resulting airfoil is pictured in Figure 3.

The remaining holes were match drilled with drill bit size 30, enlarged to size 40, deburred, and then riveted. When deburring, we left the ratchet straps on but loosened in order to save work in repositioning and tightening the straps. The complete right inboard slat is pictured below.

The right outboard slat already has a corner doubler cleco-ed and the rib assemblies were already made from the day before. We cleco-ed the ribs to 75S1-3 (the right outboard sheet), match drilled the ribs and corner doubler with 75S1-3, and then enlarged those holes. One of the holes between all three parts was not matching up correctly, and resulting in a slightly larger hole, pictured in Figure 5. We felt as though the enlargement is not much and plan to still use the same size rivet. The state of the right outboard slat at the end of this build session is pictured in Figure 6.

Work for the next build session should start with de-burring and riveting the ribs and corner doubler 75S1-3.
