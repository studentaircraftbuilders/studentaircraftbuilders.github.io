Resources

As a result of our close relationship with the University of Illinois, the Department of Aerospace, and the Institute of Aviation, we have a variety of resources at our disposal. Our early sponsorships have allowed us to obtain the materials needed to begin construction. The materials in our possession and the resources at our disposal are listed below.

Hangar

We have been granted access to a hangar at the Institute of Aviation at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign as our workshop. This hangar was specifically designed for maintenance and construction of small aircraft. As such, the hangar is wired for all electrical needs of our organization. The Institute of Aviation has generously allowed us unrestricted access to the hard piped, high flow compressor within the building. Fortunately for our builders, the hangar is temperature controlled (heating and air conditioning). In addition, there is a regularly maintained restroom very nearby. We possess 6 workbenches which will soon be mounted with benchtop machinery. This will grant us the ability to conduct concurrent construction and fully utilize the considerable personnel resources that we possess.

Tools

To date, we currently possess all of the tools vital for construction of a sheet metal aircraft. This includes:

  • Cleaveland Kit
    • This tool kit was put together based on what most homebuilders get by the time they are done building the airframe. Includes 3X Rivet Gun, C-Frame Riveting Tool, Main Squeeze with 3" Yoke, ¼" Air Drill, and other various tools and related components.
  • Air Conpressor
  • 6" Bench Grinder
  • 5" Swivel Vise with Anvil
  • 4" x 36" Belt/Disc Combination Benchtop Sander
  • 12" Ratchet Bar Clamps
  • 12" Quick Release Locking C-Clamps
  • Various hand tools

Empennage Kit

Stored in our hangar is the kit for constructing the empennage (tail) section of an RV-7a. It includes all necessary parts for the completion empennage section such as sheet metal, ribs, and hardware.

Talbot Lab

Talbot Lab, home to the Department of Aerospace Engineering, is our primary space for meetings, website development, sponsorship solicitation, recruitment, and organization.

Engineering Student Projects Lab (ESPL)

ESPL grants College of Engineering students workspace, tools, and a partial machine shop for use in extracurricular projects and activities. The machine shop includes a CNC mill, lathe, vertical band saw, and multiple drill presses. There is also a meeting room with computers, conference table, and 3' vinyl cutter.

Engineering Workstations

As University of Illinois, College of Engineering students, members of Student Aircraft Builders have 24/7 access to powerful computer stations on campus. Through these stations, members have at their disposal a wide number of useful engineering, administration, and project management software. Engineering workstations also include access to office tools such as scanners, copiers, and printers.

Grainger Engineering Library

The University of Illinois hosts the largest engineering library in the nation, Grainger Engineering Library. Through this building, Student Aircraft Builders are able to take advantage of technologically saturated group study and conference rooms.
